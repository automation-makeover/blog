Looking Towards Recovery: CBRE's 2024 Real Estate Forecast

Global real estate service provider, CBRE, predicts somewhat of a contrasting picture for the economy in 2024. While forecasting an impending reduction in the pace of economic growth, the prominent player in the industry remains optimistic as it foresees the avoidance of a recession. A concurrent expectation is a continuance in the decline of real estate transaction values.

This predicted dynamics, according to the firm's projections in the 2024 U.S. Real Estate Outlook, might imply an attractive landscape for potential investors, as purchasing opportunities become more compelling due to the dwindling transaction values.

Among the economic elements expected to pose challenges in 2024, according to CBRE, are high interest rates. Nonetheless, the firm maintains a positive outlook, asserting that strong consumer spending will serve as a form of mitigation against such economic difficulties.

Economists from CBRE place their faith in the strength of consumer spending as a device to balance out these economic challenges that may arise in 2024. Consequently, this economic resilience could act as a significant driving force towards economic recovery.

While comprehending the future can be uncertain, forecasts like these provide organizations and individuals with a strategic perspective, enabling them to plan ahead and make informed decisions. As 2024 approaches, stakeholders will anxiously watch as these predictions unfold, and hope for the early wave of recovery that CBRE anticipates within the real estate landscape.
